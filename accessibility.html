Accessibility Statement

Effective date: December 15, 2024

Making Our Platform Accessible

We want everyone to be able to use our coaching platform. As a startup, we are working to make our site accessible and will be honest about what we have done and what we are still working on.

1. Our Goal

We aim to meet basic web accessibility standards WCAG 2.1 Level AA across our main pages and tools. We design for clarity, keyboard access, and readable contrast that works with our brand colors.

2. What We Have Done

  • Used proper HTML structure with headings and labels
  • Made sure you can navigate with only a keyboard
  • Checked that our colors have good contrast for readability
  • Added descriptions for images and icons that need them
  • Kept animations subtle and respectful of motion preferences
  • Made the site work well on mobile devices and with browser zoom

3. What We Are Still Working On

We are honest about areas that need improvement:

  • Some third party tools such as payment forms may not be fully accessible yet. We are choosing the best options available
  • Our video coaching sessions are still being optimized for screen readers
  • Some interactive features might need better keyboard shortcuts
  • We are still adding captions and transcripts to all video content

4. Assistive Technology

Our site should work with common assistive technologies such as:

  • Screen readers NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver
  • Keyboard navigation
  • Browser zoom up to 200%
  • Voice control software

If something does not work with your setup, please let us know.

5. Getting Help

If you run into accessibility problems or have suggestions:

  • Email us: chizsaleei@gmail.com
  • Tell us: what page you were on and what went wrong
  • We will respond: within 3 business days
  • We will fix it: as quickly as we can, usually within a week

6. Our Commitment

As we grow, we are committed to:

  • Testing new features for accessibility before we launch them
  • Learning from user feedback and making improvements
  • Staying up to date with accessibility best practices
  • Being transparent about our progress and limitations

Accessibility is a journey, not a destination. We are on that journey and appreciate your patience as we improve.
